An Integrated Strategy for Success

Our strategy is a multi-pronged approach designed to de-risk our technology, secure funding, navigate regulations, and build a defensible market-leading position.

Targeting Flawed Supply Chains

We are launching with two "wedge" products that solve acute pain points in massive markets, demonstrating our platform's power and creating an immediate, compelling value proposition.

🦈 Squalene: "Shark-Free" Cosmetics

Address the ethical and environmental crisis of killing millions of sharks for a key cosmetic ingredient. We offer a stable, high-quality, and sustainable alternative that major brands are actively seeking.

🌲 Paclitaxel: "Forest-Free" Oncology

De-risk the supply chain for a cornerstone cancer therapy currently dependent on the slow-growing, ecologically sensitive Pacific yew tree. We provide a reliable, scalable, domestic source.

Navigating a Defined Pathway

The US regulatory framework for plant-made pharmaceuticals is well-established. Our strategy involves proactive, parallel engagement with the key agencies, building on the precedent of previously approved products.

USDA

Regulates field trials to ensure environmental and agricultural safety.

EPA

Oversees plant-incorporated protectants (pesticidal traits).

FDA

Holds final authority over the safety and efficacy of the end product.

Building a Defensible "Patent Thicket"

Our core asset is our intellectual property. We are focused on building a dense, multi-layered portfolio of utility patents—the strongest form of protection—to create a formidable competitive moat.

Composition of Matter

Patents on the novel DNA constructs and expression cassettes.

Transgenic Plants & Seeds

Patents on the engineered soybean plants, cells, and progeny.

Methods of Use & Purification

Patents on the methods for cultivation, extraction, and purification.

24-Month Implementation Roadmap

  • Complete IND-enabling studies and file IND application.
  • Conduct scaled-up cultivation in a controlled environment.
  • Secure first commercial agreement for squalene.
  • Prepare NIH SBIR/STTR application for Phase 1 trials.
